Our Story 🥐

From a tiny kitchen in Portland to your neighbourhood table — baked with love, every single day.

How It All Started

Flour Power Bakery was born in 2008 out of a cramped apartment kitchen and an obsession with the perfect sourdough loaf. Founder Maria Hartwell spent two years experimenting with wild-yeast starters before her bread earned a devoted following at the local Saturday farmers' market.

Word spread faster than warm butter on a croissant. By 2011 we had our first brick-and-mortar shop on Elm Street, and we've been growing — carefully, joyfully — ever since.

What We Believe In

🌾

Real Ingredients

No artificial preservatives — ever. We source heritage flour from family mills within 150 miles.

⏳

Slow Fermentation

Our doughs rest 18–48 hours. Good bread can't be rushed, and we refuse to hurry flavour.

♻️

Zero-Waste Baking

Day-old loaves go to local shelters. Spent grain feeds a nearby urban farm's composting programme.

🤝

Community First

We sponsor school baking clubs, host free workshops, and price every loaf so no one goes without.

Our Journey

2008

Maria perfects her 48-hour sourdough starter in a Portland apartment.

2010

First stall at the Saturday Farmers' Market — sells out in 40 minutes.

2011

Flour Power Bakery opens its Elm Street shop. The croissant waitlist begins.

2015

Launches the Community Loaf programme — pay-what-you-can pricing every Thursday.

2019

Named Best Independent Bakery by Portland Food & Drink Magazine.

2024

New production kitchen opens, tripling capacity while keeping every recipe hand-crafted.
